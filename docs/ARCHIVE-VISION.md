# Archive Vision

## Purpose

The Hooley the Poet Archive exists to preserve, organize, and explore the creative life and work of Hooley the Poet.

While songs are the primary artifacts within the archive, the archive is not merely a song catalog. It is intended to become a searchable creative record containing songs, stories, revisions, images, recordings, collections, and reflections accumulated over a lifetime of writing.

The archive should support both practical publishing needs and long-term legacy preservation.

---

## Core Principle

Songs are artifacts.

The deeper purpose of the archive is to preserve the stories, experiences, relationships, questions, dreams, disappointments, discoveries, and acts of caring that gave rise to those songs.

The archive is intended to serve as an emotional autobiography expressed through music and poetry.

---

## Intended Uses

The archive should support:

- Songbook creation
- Poetry collections
- Digital publications
- Website presentation
- SoundCloud and streaming integrations
- Future AI-assisted analysis
- Personal reflection
- Family legacy preservation
- Historical documentation of the creative process

---

## Guiding Philosophy

The archive should preserve both artistic and personal value.

Commercial success, play counts, and public popularity are useful information, but they do not determine the importance of a work.

Some songs may have significant commercial potential.

Other songs may possess deep personal significance regardless of commercial appeal.

Both forms of value deserve preservation.

---

## Core Entities

### Work

A song, poem, lyric, or other creative work.

### Version

A particular realization of a work.

### Collection

A grouping of works for a specific purpose.

A work may belong to multiple collections.

### Asset

Materials associated with a work.

Examples include cover images, audio files, video files, PDFs, and promotional artwork.

### Story

The personal history behind a work.

Examples include why it was written, where it was written, and the life circumstances surrounding its creation.

### Review

Evaluations associated with a work.

Examples include personal significance, commercial potential, emotional impact, lyrical strength, and genre fit.

---

## Legacy Goal

The archive should allow future readers, listeners, family members, and researchers to understand not only what was created, but why it mattered.

The ultimate purpose is to preserve the creative journey as faithfully as possible.

The songs tell part of the story.

The archive exists to preserve the rest.
