# Creative Workflow

## Core Insight

Hooley the Poet songs are not only music files. They are part of an emotional autobiography.

The archive should preserve the lyric, the recording, the cover art, and the story behind the song.

## Listening as Editing

Repeated listening is part of the editing process.

A song may be listened to many times, especially when newly created. During that process, small flaws may become clear, including a weak word, awkward phrase, or line that does not carry the emotional weight of the song.

The archive should support notes made while listening.

## Frustration with Version Replacement

AI-generated songs can produce a version with a particular vocal feel, arrangement, or emotional delivery that is difficult or impossible to reproduce exactly.

Sometimes the strongest musical version contains a lyric weakness. Correcting a line may require sacrificing the best performance.

The archive should preserve both:

- the best-sounding version
- the best lyric version

when they are not the same.

## Dictated Story Notes

A future mobile interface should allow the user to open a song on a phone and dictate the story behind the song.

This matters because the most useful commentary may arise while listening, driving, walking, or remembering the moment that led to the song.

The app should eventually support quick capture of:

- story behind the song
- why the song matters
- memory or life event connected to the song
- lyric revision notes
- performance notes
- version comparison notes
- book commentary ideas

## Future Book Use

The book should not merely contain lyrics.

For selected songs, it should be able to include a short personal note explaining:

- where the song came from
- what emotional question it addresses
- why it matters
- why it was selected

This allows the songbook to become an emotional autobiography without requiring a conventional autobiography.

## Design Direction

The archive should eventually include a table or content area for story notes connected to songs and song versions.

Possible future table:

- song_stories

Possible fields:

- id
- song_id
- song_version_id
- story_title
- dictated_text
- edited_text
- note_type
- emotional_theme
- book_candidate
- private_note
- created_at
- updated_at
